Sisiman Baseco

Mariveles View Park (Sisiman-Baseco), a popular tourist destination. It features a scenic overlook of Mariveles Bay. The park also has picnic areas,viewing deck, and a grassy hill. Several islands are visible in the distance, including Corregidor Island and Caballo Island. The park is open from 8:00 AM to 5:00 PM daily. Municipal Hall

The Mariveles Bataan Municipal Hall serves as the administrative center of the municipality of Mariveles, Bataan. It's a two-story structure located along Roman Superhighway in Barangay Poblacion, Mariveles. The Mariveles Municipal Hall has played a significant role in the town's history. It was built in the early 20th century and has witnessed numerous historical events, including the Second World War and the development of Mariveles as an industrial hub.

Nagbintana Cove

Nagbintana Cove, also known as Five Fingers Cove, is a small cove located in Mariveles, Bataan, Philippines. It is known for its white sand beach, clear turquoise water, and rock formations that resemble five fingers. The cove is a popular destination for swimming, snorkeling, and cliff diving. The cove is accessible by boat from Mariveles town proper. The boat ride takes about 30 minutes. There are also a few resorts located near the cove that offer overnight accommodations.

Panoypoy Spot

Due to its stunning sunset views, Panoypoy Hill has become a local favorite spot for residents of Mariveles to unwind and enjoy the natural beauty of their town. It's also become a popular tourist destination, attracting visitors from nearby areas and beyond.It's become a popular spot for locals and tourists alike, particularly in the afternoon as the sun sets, painting the sky in vibrant hues.

Mariveles Bataan

Mariveles, Bataan, is a coastal municipality at the southern tip of Bataan Peninsula, Philippines. Steeped in history and boasting stunning natural beauty, it offers a unique blend of heritage and adventure for travelers.

Mariveles is blessed with a stunning coastline, offering pristine beaches like Nagbintana Cove with its iconic "Five Fingers" rock formations, Sisiman Beach with its historic lighthouse, and secluded coves ideal for island hopping.
